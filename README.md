BudgetU: The Free Budget Tracker Built for Students, by Students
By Hirama ELIE, Aminata DIALLO & Keemyah OMOLONGO — aivancity Paris, March 2026

"I knew I was spending too much — I just had no idea where the money was actually going." — a Master's student at aivancity, January 2026

Does that sound familiar? You check your bank account mid-month and wonder how €400 disappeared in two weeks. You know you should budget, but spreadsheets feel tedious, and banking apps don't tell you much beyond a list of transactions.
You're not alone. Studies suggest that over 60% of university students run out of money before the end of the month at least once per semester — not because they earn too little, but because they have no clear picture of where their money goes.
That's exactly the problem we set out to solve.

What is BudgetU?
BudgetU is a free, mobile-first web application designed specifically for university students. It helps you track your monthly expenses, visualise your spending patterns, and receive personalised tips for cheaper alternatives — all in one place, with no account required and no app to download.
You can open it in any browser — on your phone, tablet, or laptop — and start tracking your expenses in under two minutes.

Who is it for?
BudgetU was built with one user in mind: the university student managing a tight monthly budget. Whether you're living on a student loan, a part-time salary, family support, or a mix of all three, BudgetU helps you understand your money without any financial expertise.
It's particularly useful if you:
•	Want to track expenses across categories like rent, food, transport and leisure
•	Have never used a budget tracker before and want something simple
•	Are looking for concrete tips to reduce your monthly spending
•	Want to save towards a goal — a trip, a laptop, an emergency fund

What can you do with it?
Once you open BudgetU and complete a quick 2-minute setup, you can:

📊  Track expenses — Log any transaction in seconds. The app automatically suggests a category (food, transport, rent…) based on the name you type. You can always correct it.
📈  See projections — BudgetU calculates your current daily spending rate and projects your balance at the end of the month. If you're heading for an overspend, you'll know early — not after it happens.
🔔  Get alerts — When you've spent 80% of any category budget, the app flags it. No more surprises.
💡  Discover savings — The Smart Saving Tips section suggests concrete alternatives: cheaper supermarkets (Lidl, Aldi), student canteen prices, vélo-sharing passes, cinema discounts, and more.
🎯  Set savings goals — Create a goal (e.g. "Voyage Japon – €2,000"), set a monthly contribution, and watch a progress bar fill up month by month.
🏦  Manage multiple accounts — Track your current account, savings account and credit card in one consolidated view.

Why does this matter?
Financial stress is one of the leading causes of anxiety among university students in France. Yet most financial tools are designed for professionals, not students: they're complex, subscription-based, and built around features that students simply don't need.
BudgetU is different because it was built by students, for students — with a direct understanding of what it feels like to run out of money in week three of the month.
After two months of testing with 22 students at aivancity Paris, the average satisfaction rating was 7.9 out of 10. 86% of users said the Smart Saving Tips helped them discover cheaper alternatives they were not already using.

What else is out there?
Several budgeting tools exist for general audiences, each with its own trade-offs:

YNAB (You Need A Budget) is a powerful tool, but it costs $14.99/month and has a steep learning curve. It's designed for households, not students.
Bankin' and Linxo are French apps that connect directly to your bank account. They're slick, but require sharing your banking credentials, which many students are understandably reluctant to do.
Excel or Google Sheets work well if you're disciplined, but offer no automation, no alerts, and no visual feedback that motivates behaviour change.

BudgetU fills a gap: it's free, requires no account, connects to nothing, stores data only on your device, and is specifically designed around the spending categories that matter to students.

What makes BudgetU different?
•	100% free — no subscription, no premium tier, no ads
•	No login, no data collection — your data stays on your device
•	Built mobile-first — designed for the phone in your pocket
•	Student-specific tips — not generic financial advice, but real local alternatives (canteen prices, Vélib' passes, student cinema rates)
•	AI-powered categorisation — type "Lidl" and the app already knows it's food
•	Built and tested by students at aivancity Paris
